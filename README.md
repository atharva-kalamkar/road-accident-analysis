The Road Accident Analysis dashboard reveals several important patterns and correlations within the dataset:

1. Peak Accident Time & Day Patterns

Accidents occur most frequently during late afternoon and evening hours, particularly between 4 PM â€“ 8 PM, which aligns with peak traffic and commuting periods.

Weekends show a noticeable rise in high-severity accidents, possibly due to higher speeds, leisure travel, and reduced enforcement.

2. Weather & Road Condition Impact

Clear weather accounts for the majority of total accidents due simply to frequency of occurrence, but rain, fog, and snow show a higher proportion of severe accidents, indicating reduced visibility and slippery road surfaces.

Poor road surface conditions (wet, icy, muddy) significantly correlate with increased accident severity.

4. Location & Hotspot Trends

Urban areas experience the highest accident volume due to dense traffic.

Rural and highway routes, although lower in total accidents, tend to have more severe outcomes because of higher travel speeds.

Junction types such as T-junctions, roundabouts, and crossroads show elevated accident counts.

5. Vehicle Type Contribution

Cars are involved in the majority of incidents, consistent with vehicle density.

However, motorcycles and heavy vehicles contribute disproportionately to fatal-level severity, highlighting vulnerability and kinetic impact forces.

6. Temporal Trends

Accidents peak during late months of the year, where weather worsens and days get shorter.

Seasonal patterns indicate that winter months correlate with more severe collisions.

7. Human & Environmental Factors

Speeding, distraction, and failure to judge vehicle movement appear as recurring contributing factors.

Environmental influences such as dark lighting conditions, curvy road design, and poor signage further elevate risk.
